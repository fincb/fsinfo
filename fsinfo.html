Underwater Acoustic Time Series Simulation using Fourier Synthesis

Real sonar time series data is limited and hard to come by, therefor an accurate means to simulate it is crucial. This infographic contains further details, examples and a means to listen to the results.

1
Motivation
  • Algorithm testing — Beamforming, detection, and tracking operate on time‑domain signals.
  • Sonar performance — Time‑domain signals reveal pulse spreading and multipath arrivals.
  • Model inputs — Machine learning and matched filtering require realistic simulated time series.
2
Advantages
  • Computational expense — Frequency‑domain modelling avoids direct time stepping.
  • Parallelism — Independent frequency components can be computed in parallel.
  • Solver agnostic — Works with any propagation model, e.g. ray tracing, normal modes, parabolic equation.

          5
          Conclusion

          Fourier synthesis enables realistic simulation of underwater acoustic time series, supporting algorithm development and performance analysis. By leveraging efficient frequency-domain models in parrallel accurate time-domain signals can be generated for various sonar applications. Hopefully the examples above demonstrate the dramatic effects of reverberation and multipath in underwater acoustics, and the need for accurate simulation.
